Women Empowerment

Our aim is to make a substantial change in the condition of women
who are victims of abuse and violence in Singapore with
an ultimate mission to achieve equal recognition and safety for females
as well as to encourage the ideals of feminism.

In collaboration with SCWO

Equal Space

SCWO believes men and women in Singapore should have equal space in the home, institution, workplace and community. Women should be able to live in a safe space, without having to fear being discriminated against, or fear violence and threats to their safety.

Equal Voice

Women should have the right to speak about issues affecting them and be equally represented in leadership positions. Women, regardless of their social status, marital status and jobs should be equally heard.

Equal Worth

All women should be equally recognized for their contributions to society and be respected and treated equally. Disadvantaged women such as single mothers and migrant spouses should have squal rights and be given the same benefits as other mothers.

#PurplePower

Why HuesOfPurple?

We came up with the name "Hues of Purple" because we have a vision of accelerating the integration of vulnerable women into society.

The color purple has been a symbol of the women's movement since the early 1900s when it became an official color of the Suffragettes campaigning to gain the right to vote. It symbolizes acheivements gained and acheivements yet to come. Also, purple is quite an amazing color regardless!

Why should you Donate?

For every $25, you can sponsor the transportation costs of a survivor to help her go to work or send her child to school.
For every $50, you can sponsor a session for a therapeutic programme to help a survivor heal from the trauma of abuse.
For every $100, you can sponsor an employability enhancement workshop for up to 10 survivors.
For every $250, you can sponsor the meals of 30 survivors in a month.
