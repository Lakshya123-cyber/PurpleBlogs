The Golden Era of Harassment in Bollywood

10 year old Jivika would've never recognized the flaws her “favourite rom-com” had until she became an avid researcher once she turned 17. Hi, I'm Jivika, a student in singapore, and a not so proud DDLJ fan once I started collating data for this article. Let's start off with my used to be favourite rom com.

"Maine apko kahin dekha hai"
"Yeh Joh apki ankhen hai" says Shahrukh Khan while getting uncomfortably close to Kajol, in one of the famous train scenes in a "Bollywood classic" Dil Wale Dhulhaniya Le Jayenge or more popularly known as "DDLJ".
It is just another movie depicting the true ideal: harass her till she says yes. This particular scene is displayed as "flirty" while we can clearly see Kajol uncomfortable as she also mentions multiple times, "Please leave me alone!"
This "flirty" nature is now promoted among many young men in India and thus these cases of real-life harassment are either overlooked or just another taunt at the victim. I truly apologise for ruining one of your childhood memories but in Demi Lovato's quote: Sorry not Sorry, and I'll continue to shatter a few more.

Now before calling me out and labelling me as a "woke Indian" by mentioning "please stop ruining DDLJ with your cringeworthy 2020 opinion" as one of the most reputed Indian media outlet The Print mentioned and of course provided justification of the scene, saying the motivation behind it was "to paint flawed, but human characters", so hear me out and let me describe multiple scenes from different movies; the real-life influence of such movies on the youth of India and their outstanding promotion of harassment normalisation. Now one fact I hope my readers and I can agree upon is the portrayal of harassment is subtle and "cute" in these movies. It is even romanticized to the extent that the naked human eye can see bacteria on food but not the harassment.

Let's take another example from 2019, not a classic but definitely a blockbuster: “Kabir Singh”. It is the pioneer in normalizing an abusive relationship. When Sandeep Reddy the director asked the question about our protagonist Kabir slapping Preeti he responded, I kid you not, that the physical demonstration is symptomatic of being vested in a relationship.
He said that women who found this portrayal offensive haven't experienced this "kind of love" and have probably never loved. Do you see some jarring offensive comments here? Not convinced yet? Let's take a look at an even more recent movie, Love Aaj Kal released in 2020 in which Imtiaz Ali has taken the liberty to create a glorified montage of stalking the second the film opens, Kartik Aryan follows around Sara Ali Khan, and Aarushi Gupta in two different love stories in different timelines.
For the film whose purpose was to display modern romance we can, safely say that it has miserably failed due to an orthodox director with an even more orthodox approach which is reflected in its apt IMDB score of 4.6/10. At least such an IMBD score represents to an extent that while stalker behavior might still be the norm on the reel, the Indian audience is evolving past it in real life. Similarly Raanjhnaa where a school boy stalks a school girl and they eventually fall in love. A little spoiler alert for you who are planning to adopt a similar approach - women do not like being followed. Now you might say what is the evidence that even if such movies are created they have the power to influence an individual?

Well, unfortunately, there is more than sufficient evidence to support this, a 32-year-old Indian man in Australia was accused of stalking two women one for 18 months and another for 4 months. He escaped conviction on the grounds of "cultural Bollywood influence" Still not convinced? Well, let me remind you of a basic slang thrown around by avid Bollywood fans and maybe even you without realising its meaning, "Item song", "Item girl", - objectifying women at its finest reducing them to mere "items" for the male gaze. This term is thrown around for any Bollywood song depicting a sensuous sexy woman dancing around multitudes of men.
Calling her an item, does it ring a bell now? I hope it does because as a society unless we don't change, we don't hold popular directors, writers, and actors accountable,harassment will find its way to our cinemas and OTT platforms.

My sincere request is to continue watching and enjoying Bollywood movies as many of them created a positive impact on our Indian society and have been catalysts in transforming some of our outdated mindsets. However, be aware and try raising your voice, or take small steps - by sharing low ratings and critical reviews on such movies. This ought to notify our content creators that we are not "cringeworthy youth of 2022" but well-informed, original creative not-so-misogynistic content consumers.
