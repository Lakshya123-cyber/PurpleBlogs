The Timeless Significance of
Red Shoes

Red is a color usually associated with deep burning desire. Red shoes have been symbolic of many emotions throughout history - burning desire and vitality, courage and love, religion, and even more. Red shoes, which have been seen on the feet of ballerinas, aristocracy, popes, and pop stars, have sparked imaginations, stoked outbursts, and elicited mesmerizing and scandalous looks.

From representing nobility to sin and now to representing feminism, here's a look at the timelessly significant journey of red shoes which have made substantial appearances throughout history.

17th century

In the era of the Great Turbulence, red shoes indicated POWER AND NOBILITY, similar to Louboutins indicating wealth. This is evident, especially in France, where France's 17th-century King Louis XIV was seen posing with red heels in a painting. Heels demonstrated that the wearer was wealthy enough to avoid scuffing them up and strong enough to tramp down his foes. This meaning was emphasized more when the heel was in red.

18th century

The 18th through 19th century was a dark era for red shoes. Red shoes were incorporated in books and films with not the best meaning. To pick an example from a popular story - What started as a seemingly normal fairytale around a girl named Karen was all fun until the girl manages to obtain red leather shoes. Her desire for something as simple as eye-catching footwear appears to be so grotesque that she is denigrated by an angel to dance herself to tragedy. Obviously enough, it does not make the best bedtime story. It's a horrifyingly macabre little story, made entirely of unsavory messages about vanity's punishment. This gruesome story was adapted somewhat around the similar plot of Kate Bush's 1993 album The Red Shoes.

Current Times

The modern era has been the finest for red shoes, with them having the most positive and empowering meaning. Aside from being a bold fashion statement, it now also represents feminism and anti-femicide. The significance of red shoes was added by Elina Chauvet, who's known famously for her red shoes. According to an oft-cited statistic, on average, 10 women and girls are killed each day in Mexico. The artist emoted femicide by using red shoes representing the pain and passion to stop the terrible cause. Not just one, but thousands of pairs of red boots, heels, and toddler shoes were made by her. They are there since the women or girls who would have worn them no longer occupy any space other than in the lives of their loved ones. This movement was a result of Chauvet's “Los Zapatos Rojos” installation in cities around the world. This story tells the burning passion and beautiful yet sad meaning behind the mesmerizing and luxurious red shoes. Red had a lot of significance for Chauvet. According to the artist, it represents bloodshed but also change, hope, and love.
