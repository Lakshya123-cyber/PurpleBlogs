Rape Culture: Conceding to the reality of society

A United Nations statistical report compiled from the government sources of 65 countries revealed that more than 250,000 cases of rape/attempted rape were recorded by police annually. The number provided above covers only the "reported cases" while in reality, this number is quite high, as many victims are scared to come out with their assault stories for they won't be believed and the onus of the crime would somehow be put on them.

This fear is not futile. Around every corner of the world, a culture exists that is intoxicating the lives of the victims and threatening them to behave submissively and confirm the century-old patriarchal rules. Unfortunately and unknowingly we become the propagators of this tradition, which is quite reflective in our behaviors and actions. For example, Victim-blaming, Slut-shaming, Denial of widespread rape under false accusations, and sexual objectification are the kinds of behaviors portrayed toward victims.

THE POINTING FINGERS

"She was wearing such short clothes. Nowadays, girls dress indecently and then expect men to not get excited? Such whores!",

"Hey, she is a prostitute. I mean that's her work! Maybe she wants some extra money from the client, that's why she is accusing him of rape!",

"She wants to become the boss, that's her tactic of stealing that position!",

"Look, she hangs out with boys, laughs out so loudly with them.. it would be no surprise if something happens with her, she is clearly sending signals."

All of us at some point or another have heard or even said these things for the victims. Victim blaming the survivor legitimizes and excuses the crime that should be considered inexcusable. Victim blaming instills the fear of judgment and mistrust in people who survived abuse so much so that they continue to bear the trauma alone or have suicidal ideation.

MEANS OF PROMOTING RAPE CULTURE

Regrettably, the conservative mindsets and media have largely indulged in promoting the rape culture. Here's how.

REVENGE PORN

Revenge porn is when a person's explicit photos/videos are uploaded on the internet without their consent generally by an intimate partner to blackmail the person to perform sexual acts on-demand, come into a relationship, or punish them for not doing/behaving a certain way.
Such images are largely available on the internet and direct the message to the viewers that an excusable way to blackmail a person into doing something is to rape and film them.

MOVIES

Films have a strong influence on the minds of people. Fans, including small children, imitate the role and ideologies of their favorite actors. This mandates the accountability and responsibility on part of filmmakers, actors, writers, producers, and censor boards to be vigilant enough about the content they are putting out for the audience.

Movies and dramas including renowned Thai-drama Tra Barb See Chompoo, Praomook; popular K-Dramas like Boys over Flowers, Playful Kiss; animes; Bollywood movies like Kabir Singh, Dabbang, and many others at various scenes incorporate casual sexism, misogyny, along with normalizing assaults, stalking and men forcing themselves upon women to legitimize their toxicity under the garb of showing love. Movies and series like them dismiss the concept of CONSENT at large and direct the audience towards the wrong path.

SONGS

"I don't really care how long you've been together with your man. It's just a matter of time before I make you mine. I wanna make it clear so there's no misunderstandings, that I get what I want, when I want." [If I Want To by Usher 2001]

"I'll scream with you: Ah! somebody help! Don't you get it b**ch? No one can hear you. Now shut the f***k up, and get what's coming to you. You were supposed to love me, now bleed, b**ch , bleed." [Kim by Eminem 2000]

There is no conflict that women indeed are in a lamentable state for they have always been sexualized. They have always been seen as an object to attain sexual desires by men, and if they dare say NO, the punishment is Rape.

Songs like them are impacting the mindset of people including young boys who might grow up inculcating this toxic ideology.

LEARNED SEXISM AND TOXICITY

Parents, grandparents, teachers, and other adults that child has direct contact with, do influence tender minds in addition to media. The sexism prevalent in homes and schools unknowingly gets instilled so deeply in children that they might foster the contaminating patriarchal mindsets and misogyny and in turn harm society. Children growing up in a traditional conservative patriarchal family, from a young state, start to employ the feeling of envy and disgust for girls and women out there who try to break the societal laws and live lives on their terms. For example, many times we have come across this line: "You cannot work. No woman in my family ever worked! I won't allow you. It's a matter of pride for us. We cannot allow a woman in our family to work outside. If you want to work, work in the house, do household chores, who is stopping you?" (A classic instance of inherited patriarchy!)

Additionally, at school, moral policing girls for wearing skirts above the knee, having friendships with boys, or sexualizing them for periods promote gender disparity in a place that should be equal for everyone. Saying things like “Your skirt is very short! Are you trying to attract boys?” incorporates self-guilt in women as they think it was their fault that they were assaulted.

It thus becomes more crucial to impart sex education from an early stage, censor the content that can promote rape culture, promote mindful parenting and create a safe space for every gender be it a woman, man, bi-sexual, gender fluid, pansexual, or others. Every one of us shall remember that this culture is propagated by us and we are the ones who can stop it. After all, Change begins at the end of your comfort zone.
